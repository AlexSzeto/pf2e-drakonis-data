# Reflection
Roll 1d4. The PC can choose to experience the flashback or resist it. End result is the same: 
Will save 20 2d6 psychic damage, unlock Hero Abilities:

## Hero Abilities (Spend 1 Hero Point)
- Heighten a spell (+1)
- Second Wind (non-casters): instantly restore 1d8 hitpoints
- Cast a mental trait spell on creatures immune to it. Spell DC -2

## Grom's Reflection
- Location: PC's choice or Hawkridge if within Uruzad, Felkis in Emeralis if outside Uruzad)
- Person: Yukha, from the Order of PC's choice or the Granite Fist
- Event: A street brawl ends in an invitation to join the order, to protect the innocent
Renu's Remark:

> You mistake chaotic violence for righteous purpose, brute. Your 'order' is just a gang of street thugs trading one master for another. True peace only comes when the fist is guided by a single, unified mind.

Xovina's Encouragement:

> Do not listen to the tyrant's despair, Grom. A shield can be forged from pristine steel, but also from bruised knuckles and blood. You protect the vulnerable with raw, chaotic devotion, and in that, there is pure divinity.

## Lemmy's Reflection
- Location: Uruzad desert
- Person: Touman
- Event: The two of you seek a secret oasis in the midst of a drought. With Touman's encouragement,
you unlock your bardic talent with song and walked out of the desert alive.

Xovina's Encouragement:

> "A single, defiant note can shatter the deepest silence. Your song birthed an oasis in the barren wastes, Lemmy. Never let him tell you that hope is a delusion; it is the truest magic we possess."

# The Viewing Chamber
The Tear is an illusion - DC 26 perception to disbelieve without interaction

The illusion tear is protected by a magical barrier - Hardness 5 HP 20. Level 5/Spell DC20 to dispel.
Any damage to the barrier would trigger the armor golems.

The mechanisms to play the melody and unlock either secret door is at the base of the pedestal.
Lori taught the PCs how to reopen the path to the hidden fort. It takes Arcana DC 23 to study the mechanism
and understand that there are at least three variations programmed into the mechanism:

- secret door to the fort
- secret door to shadow labs
- arm/disarm the barrier, which would also reset the golems

Tighis' vision gives the viewer a 50/50 to play a melody to reset the golems. Playing any melody takes 3 actions.

# The Meditation Chamber
Silence is cast over the chamber, preventing verbal communication. 

# the Truthseeker (Tighis) Chamber (left)
The true combination for arming/disarming the golems can be found here. Tighis suspects foul play but was not able to
figure out Renu's true plans.

# The Shepard's (Iloth) Chamber (center)
Filled with sweets. Nothing of import here.

# The Oathkeeper's (Nyzassa) chambers
Journal entries about Renu's increasing paranoia, eventually stopping the ceremonies to open the Tears for viewing. Though
the passage to the chambers are silenced, The Oathkeeper hears vibrations at regular hours convincing her that a chord
is being played regularly at odd hours. DC 15 performance reveals the chord combination to play to open the secret passage.

# Shadow Labs
DC 23 to spot the purple slime.

The Clues: Scattered around the edges of the room are the Prince's frantic, final workstations. The PCs need to find these to understand the final room's mechanics:

The Worm Terrariums: Dead worms pile on the altar.  Notes describe them as the "perfect vessels—able to bypass any physical barrier."

The Slime Vats: Notes on the purple slime's unique properties. "Distance means nothing. Cut it into a thousand pieces, and it remains one mind. It will bind the swarm."

The Ritual Blueprint: On the table, a massive schematic of the room below. It shows three distinct "Nodes" feeding into a central platform. The notes read: "The Tear provides the spark. The Slime provides the mind. The Worms provide the vessel. Nyzassa will provide the direction. The perfect infiltrator, born of four parts."
