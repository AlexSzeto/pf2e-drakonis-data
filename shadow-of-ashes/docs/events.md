# Shadow Labs
**DC 23 to spot the purple slime.**

The Clues: Scattered around the edges of the room are the Prince's frantic, final workstations. The PCs need to find these to understand the final room's mechanics:

### The Worm Terrariums
Dead worms pile on the altar.  Notes describe them as
>  the perfect vessels—able to bypass any physical barrier.

### The Slime Vat
Notes on the purple slime's unique properties.
> Distance means nothing. Cut it into a thousand pieces, and it remains one mind. It will bind the swarm.

### The Ritual Blueprint
On the table, a massive schematic of the room below. It shows three distinct "Nodes" feeding into a central platform. The notes read:
> The Tear provides the spark. The Slime provides the mind. The Worms provide the vessel. An intelligent mind will provide the direction. The perfect infiltrator, born of four parts.

On a journal entry:
> Nyzassa is too close to the truth. Will lay a trap for her. Her sacrifice will be fitting to complete my ritual.

## Slime Ambush

The purple slime waits on the ceiling and jumps down on the first person to go under it, immediately trying to engulf the person. Through Xovina's blessing the engulfed person can hear the slime's thoughts:

> Torn apart and forcefully rejoined... endless suffering... you sentients are cruel... must destroy all...

**DC 25 to convince the purple slime to leave the PCs in peace. If reduced to half health or below, DC 20 intimidate to make the purple slime run away.**

# Ritual Chamber

Nyzassa and two Arcane Guardians protect the ritual.

> It is my duty to keep the Oath and protect the flock...

**Nyzassa can be convinced DC 22 to stop helping Renu and providing memory (her arguing with Renu at the meeting hall, and the journal entry about Renu's trap) each decrease the DC by 2**

## The Ritual

The outer nodes power the ritual. The barrier around Renu and the Tear is unbreakable until the other components are destroyed. Renu has 54hp but heals to full every round on his initiative.

The lines connecting the nodes and the circle around each node are void empowered. Entering the line, or ending a turn next to a ritual circle deals 1d6 void damage.

To destroy each node, the magical barrier (Level 5/Spell DC 20 to dispel or Hardness 5 HP 20) must be destroyed, and a single action spent to destroy the node by removing the material components.

The arcane golems are directly controlled by Nyzassa. They cannot be broken by using the normal command words.

> Interlopers - whether you are petty thieves or misguided fools who think what you are doing is for the greater good, you have no idea what it means to suffer as our people had - lost, abandoned, betrayed. Know that you are snuffing the last hope for our people to make a difference in this world again. You. Personally. 

## Destroying the purple slime node first

The ritual destabilize and the brown maggots are empowered, destroying all creatures in Renu's circle and spawning 3 Brown Maggots near his body.

## Destroying the brown maggots node first

The ritual destabilize and the purple slimes are empowered, destroying all creatures in Renu's radius and spawning 3 Purple Slime Spawns near his body.

## Destroying Prince Renu first

The ritual immediately destabilize and the other nodes explode, triggering the Xovina's Wish event.

## Leaving Prince Renu for last

The ritual breaks, and Prince Renu makes a last stand by dominating Nyzassa one last time to proect his body.

## Shattering the ritual

As Prince Renu dies, all PCs make a perception check to notice that the Tears would fall to the ground (DC 20). If anyone makes the check, initiative resets to the roll that the PCs made and the PC with the highest perception can then take a full turn to try and move underneath the tears and ready an action to try and catch it (acrobatics DC 18).

Any PC who touches the Tear, takes 2d6 psychic damage. If the PC is still conscious, they can try to interact with the Tear with the following skills. All DCs are 22:
- Arcana, Occultism: try to manipulate the Tear as a magical artifact
- Diplomacy, Religion: try to communicate with the soul of Xovina

If the PC fails, the Tear rejects the PC and they fall unconscious immediately. Other PCs may attempt to touch the Tear and activate it.

If the Tear falls to the ground, it shatters, releasing powerful magical energy.

Starting from the second round, the chamber begins to collapse from the uncontrolled enegry of the artifact and the collapse of the ritual - all PCs must make an athletics or acrobatics DC 16 check at the start of each round or take 1d6 damage from falling debris.

# Xovina's Wish

A PC who makes the check successfully connects to Xovina's soul - they are now the only person who can use the artifact.

> We don't have much time. The prince had twisted my power to its limit, and the energy channeled through the ritual must be released. I am, by design, a vessel of power without a will. As my new rightful master you must now take control of my power. What is your wish, mortal?

On a success, the PCs can carry out a limited wish with Xovina's approval. On critical success, the PC can exert their will on Xovina and almost anything is possible.

Once the wish is complete, the chamber resets, releasing a torrent of water energy and reverting the soul of Xovina inside the Tear into her child form. She will remain dormant for an unknown amount of time.

# Epilogue

If there are survivors revived inside Xyndrasis, the PCs find Nerkith and the other Ashlings wandering through the city. Nerkith had a change of heart. After locating the remaining survivors. They decide to leave Xyndrasis and reconnect with the world.

If the undead citizens of Xyndrasis are obliterated or cannot be controlled, the PCs find the survivors at Keroc.

Either way, the ashlings make camp at Keroc. If the PCs stay, they witness the ashlings perform night wake under the stars, with Yi'tha leading the singing.

Nerkith thanks the PCs for restoring his faith in trusting outsiders. Hethor thanks the PCs for bringing the next generation hope.

Yi'tha thanks the PCs, but raises her concern about Xara's true intention:

> Now that I can finally stop worrying and start thinking for once, I've been thinking... Xara sent all of you to retrieve the Tear of Xovina for her, not knowing that the city was in ruins. If the city was as she remembered and you've stolen the Tear from us, then we would become helpless in more ways than one. Xara can be... manipulative, but I've not known her to be cruel like my father once was. Something doesn't add up. I must see her as soon as possible, if only to find what her true intentions are.

The PCs remember that the Burning Hand are still at large, after their setback. As the dawn breaks and they return to Stormhaven, they see smoke in the horizon, deep within the city - it still stands, but it appears that all the trouble at Uruzad are far from over.
